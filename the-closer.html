Our Door is Always Open

On January 12, I hosted the first-ever Economic Development Cluster Open House featuring all of the agencies of the DMPED cluster in the District of Columbia. 

It was the first time where all 14 of our cluster agencies, their leadership and staff were in one place to greet residents, answer questions and share information about our services.

Given we hadn’t done this before, we weren’t sure what to expect. But you all came out – and in big numbers. More than 500 residents, business owners, employees, ANCs, employees, stakeholders (and even a few babies!) came out to see us over the four-hour period at the Convention Center. 

We can’t thank you enough for being engaged in our economic development work for the District of Columbia. I also want to thank the DMPED cluster agencies for their hard work in making the event such a success. As I often tell my staff: “Public service is a privilege,” but we can’t do the work without your input.

The intent of the open house was to set a tone for the year – we are here for you. Our door is always open. Please continue to let us know how we are doing and share your great ideas for the District.  

Regards,

Deputy Mayor Brian T. Kenner

Interactive Language Museum Coming to Franklin School

DMPED made the exciting announcement last week that Ann B. Friedman and Dantes Partners will lead the redevelopment of the historic Franklin School, located at the intersection of 13th Street NW and K Street NW in Ward 2. Delivering on the Bowser Administration’s commitment to the creative economy, the proposed development will transform the historic space into Planet Word, DC’s first-ever interactive language arts museum and education space. Planet Word’s mission is to inspire a love of words and language and increase literacy through a host of interactive exhibits and experiences. Along with the Mundolingua in Paris, Planet Word will be one of just a few fully-operational language museums globally. Learn more about Planet Word at this link. The museum plans to open its doors to the public by winter 2019. To view related articles and social media, follow our Storify link. 

Autonomous vehicles in DC! Who's ready to roll?

Could driver-less cars be the future of mobility and transportation in the District of Columbia? DMPED wants to find out. In early January, Bloomberg Philanthropies and the Aspen Institute named the District of Columbia alongside other global cities to help produce a set of principles and tools that cities can use to plan for the future of driverless or “autonomous vehicle” technologies. 

In addition, as part of the Washington Auto Show MobilityTalks International Form on Jan. 24, Deputy Mayor Kenner (as seen in above photo) discussed autonomous vehicles with Senator Gary Peters (D-MI) and Ms. Jean Shiomto, the Administrator of the California DMV. The panel focused on how state and local governments can best interact with federal officials to keep consumers safe without stifling innovation as autonomous cars emerge on the roads. Kenner shared that the District is eager to find out if autonomous vehicles could solve issues in D.C. such as improving traffic conditions, reducing traffic fatalities and providing transportation options in underserved areas, especially as our population continues to grow. Stay tuned for more to come on the District’s work on autonomous driver technologies.   

District First City to Have Food Bots

Coming soon to a door near you: food bots! At the Consumer Electronics Show, Jan. 5-8, in Las Vegas, Nevada, Starship Technologies announced that the District of Columbia is the pilot city for autonomous food delivery bots. The company, formed in 2014 by Janus Friis and Ahti Heinla, two Skype cofounders, aims to revolutionize the local food delivery sector and bring the cost of delivery to $1 or less. Seen here Deputy Mayor Brian Kenner and Director of Business Development and Strategy Karima Woods checking out the food bot technology with the founders. Each robot can carry up to 40 lbs (or about two grocery bags) and travel within a three-mile radius. The bots are in use in England, Germany and Switzerland and already covered 14,500 miles. You may see the food bots around D.C. as early as this fall. Read more about the food bot technology at this link.

Capitol Hill Community Enagages in OurRFP Process

On Jan. 28, more than 40 residents dedicated part of their Saturday to provide DMPED, the Office of Planning and Councilmember Charles Allen (Ward 6) with their input on the future of the former site of the Eastern Branch Boys and Girls Club at 261 17th St SE. 

The site is an “OurRFP” project, a Mayoral Initiative that incorporates community engagement early in the Request for Proposals (RFP) process. Our goal is to ensure that the public’s perspective and priorities are understood in conjunction with the District’s goals when crafting and issuing the RFP for this District-owned parcel.

At the event, DMPED actively solicited feedback on how to shape the future development of this site from residents and community members in the form of a facilitated break-out group session. Those who couldn’t make the event are encouraged to provide their feedback in our Online Engagement Forum through Feb. 15. Also, make plans to join us for a follow-up meeting on Feb. 16 at 6:30 p.m. at Friendship Chamberlain Elementary School (1345 Potomac Ave, SE) where we will reconnect with the community on the feedback received further refine major community preferences, and explain how community input will be incorporated in the RFP. To read more on this project, follow this link.

Affordable Housing Spotlight

The District Department of Housing and Community Development (DHCD) closed a loan last week with the NHP Foundation that will preserve 148 affordable housing units at Benning Heights Apartments in the Ward 7 neighborhood of Fort Dupont Park. As a result of the financing efforts, all 148 units will remain affordable to households earning no more than 60 percent of the Area Median Income (AMI) for at least the next 40 years. The unit mix will accommodate both individuals and families, consisting of 54 one-bedroom units, 84 two-bedroom units and 10 three-bedroom units. Since January 2015, DHCD has produced and preserved over 2,100 affordable housing units in the District with more to come. Another 5,300 affordable housing units—capable of housing nearly 12,000 District residents—are in the development pipeline.

DMPED "Closings"

Franklin School: As mentioned above, the historic Franklin School will be the home of a one-of-a-kind museum dedicated to language called Planet Word. The new museum will provide a grand total of nearly 15,000 sf of exhibits space, a 2,000 sf state-of-the-art auditorium, a 3,500 sf restaurant and 1,900 sf in classroom space. The over $30 million development is projected to generate 35 new full-time jobs and welcome nearly 100,000 visitors annually. What a great addition to the D.C. museum scene! 

Walter Reed: On January 13, Director of the Walter Reed LRA Randall Clarke joined Ward 4 Councilmember Brandon Todd on a tour of the new DC International School/ LAMB PCS building on the Walter Reed campus. This $56 million redevelopment and expansion of historic Delano Hall will ultimately house as many as 1,700 children from all eight wards of the District. The schools plan to open in the fall for the 2017/2018 school year.

Data Corner: DC Unemployment Rate Down 1.7 Percent with Mayor Bowser

As you know, DMPED loves data. So you can probably guess that we were excited to see the latest labor statistics.

The District of Columbia Department of Employment Services (DOES) reported last week that the seasonally adjusted preliminary unemployment rate was 5.8 percent in December – down 0.2 percentage points from the reported November unemployment rate, down 0.7 percentage points from one year ago, and down 1.7 percentage points since Mayor Muriel Bowser was sworn into office on January 5, 2015.

Fun fact: private sector employment makes up nearly 70% of the DC job market (see below chart).

Based on more complete reporting from employers, previously released November estimates were revised upward to show an over-the-month (October 16- November 16) total non-farm employment increase of 1,700 jobs. Now these are stats that we love hearing about at DMPED!
